A high-throughput method for assessing gene expression throughout the transcriptome is bulk RNA sequencing. It is essential to comprehending the biology of disease, particularly in the study of cancer. A comprehensive and openly accessible database, the Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) provides extensive genomic datasets, including RNA-seq and clinical metadata for different forms of cancer.
The epigenetic regulator TET2 (Ten-Eleven Translocation 2) is known to regulate DNA demethylation. Its loss-of-function mutations or altered expression, linked to a number of malignancies, affect immune regulation and patient prognosis. Investigating the downstream transcriptional effects of TET2 expression levels and their relationship to immune cell infiltration is the main goal of this project.
The main goal of this project was to use TCGA RNA-seq data to look into how TET2 expression affects the immune system in cancer. Based on the amount of TET2 present, patient samples were divided into two groups: TET2-high (above the 80th percentile) and TET2-low (below the 20th percentile). This was followed by immune cell deconvolution using the CIBERSORT method to guess the numbers of different types of immune cells in each sample. Then, a differential gene expression study was done between the TET2-high and TET2-low groups to find genes that had changed a lot. Finally, the information from the tests was shown in the form of helpful graphs and figures. The goal was to find trends related to immune modulation and transcriptional differences linked to TET2 expression.
